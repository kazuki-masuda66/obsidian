Let me start with the space situation for **Week 44**.

AX1 and AX3 are expected to depart full in **TEU basis**, while AX2 and AX4 are expected to be full in **weight basis**.

The total **roll pool** for Week 44 is about **1,673 TEUs**, with **misconnections** estimated at **487 TEUs**.

For **Week 45**, AX1 is expected to be full in TEU basis, and AX2 and AX3 are expected to be full in weight basis.

As for AX4, although there are many bookings, the **show-up ratio** has worsened.

Based on the provisional show-up ratio provided by ECM, the current estimate is **88% on TEU basis** and **74% on weight basis**.

A shortfall of about **200 TEUs** is expected on AX4, but we plan to fill up this with the roll pool from other AX services.

We have also pushed the Front Offices to check the status of **unpicked containers**, and we are closely monitoring whether this indicates a slowdown.

Currently, the **roll pool** stands at **1,000 TEUs**, and **misconnections** at **90 TEUs**.

(The slowdown after **Week 45** is because the **ETA falls after the RR period**.)

Next, for **Week 46**, vessels during this period will start arriving around **Christmas and New Year**.

There will be **no AX4** this week.

AX1 is expected to be full on a TEU basis, while AX2 and AX3 are expected to be full on a weight basis.

The **roll pool** is projected to reach **1,180 TEUs**.
